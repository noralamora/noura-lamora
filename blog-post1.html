Translating the Fantastical: Challenges and Joys

August 10, 2025

Article

Bringing fantasy and science‑fiction into Arabic requires more than a simple exchange of words; it demands a deep immersion in two worlds of culture and imagination. Al‑Jāḥiẓ famously observed that a translator cannot fully convey an author’s intent unless he equals the original writer in understanding and mastery【209688961013450†L21-L27】. He insisted that the translator must know both languages equally well, for when two languages meet in one tongue, each influences the other【209688961013450†L29-L33】. These warnings resonate with me whenever I approach a new project.

When translating fantastical elements, the challenge lies in balancing fidelity with readability. Terms like “wizard,” “dragon” or “spaceship” often have no direct equivalents in classical Arabic. I search the lexicons of our tradition and the richness of the Arabic language to find words that evoke the same wonder without betraying the source. In doing so, I remember al‑Jāḥiẓ’s conviction that the translator must not let the two languages dilute each other【209688961013450†L31-L33】; instead, each phrase must be rendered with care to preserve its meaning and tone.

There is joy in this craft. Creating a phrase that marries the rhythm of Arabic prose with the imagery of an English fantasy novel is an art form. My training in the eloquence of al‑Jāḥiẓ guides me; he valued clarity, precision and charm in language【560246731437962†L60-L66】. By drawing on his approach, I aim to give Arabic readers the same sense of wonder that captivated me when I first encountered these stories.

«الترجمة ليست إحلال كلمة مكان كلمة فحسب، بل هي جسر بين ثقافتين وإبداعين. حين أنقل عوالم الخيال إلى لغتنا أستحضر قول الجاحظ بأن الترجمان لا يؤدي ما قاله الحكيم إلا إذا كان علمه في وزن علمه【209688961013450†L21-L27】، وأن على المترجم أن يكون أعلم الناس باللغتين حتى لا يدخل الضيم عليهما【209688961013450†L29-L33】. في كل مشروع جديد أجد متعةً في البحث عن مفردات عربية قديمة تعبر عن السحر والدهشة، فأصوغ بها جملةً تليق ببلاغة الجاحظ، وتمنح القارئ العربي متعة الخيال الأصيلة».
